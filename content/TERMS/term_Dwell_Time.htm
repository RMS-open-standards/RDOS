Dwell Time

The dwell time modifier captures the average duration (in days) that a marine risk stays at a port or geocoded location. Unlike property risks that are at risk 365 days a year, cargo is at risk only when it is present at an insured location. Together with number of shipments, the dwell time attribute considers the transient nature of marine risks and enables accurate loss estimation. Dwell time is one of the most important considerations impacting port concentrations (the amount of time cargo spends in port). Many factors influence dwell time, including the size and type of cargo, port automation, regional cargo practices, demurrage fees, and import/export ratios. The range for the dwell time attribute is 0-365 days.

If information about cargo dwell time at a port or location is not available, the value for dwell time should be equal to 1 (the default). For the value 1, the marine model ignores this attribute and does not perform any temporal risk adjustment, which means that marine risk is assumed to be present at the geocoded location throughout the year, similar to property risk. This field is applicable only for modeling marine cargo and specie exposures.
